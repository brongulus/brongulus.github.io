First Fundamental Form and Local Intrinsic Properties of a Surface Introduction The surfaces are defined similar to curves by an equation of the type F(x,y,z) = 0 or parametrically by expressing x,y,z in terms of two parameters u,v varying over a domain. After defining the surface locally, its points are classified as ordinary or singular. Then using tangent plane at a point and the surface normal at it, a coordinate system \((r_1, r_2, N)\) at every point of the surface is introduced.
